Rolihlahla Mandela was born into the Madiba clan in the village of Mvezo,
in the Eastern Cape, on 18 July 1918. His mother was Nonqaphi Nosekeni and his father was Nkosi
Mphakanyiswa Gadla Mandela, principal counsellor to the Acting King of the Thembu people, Jongintaba
Dalindyebo. In 1930, when he was 12 years old, his father died and the young Rolihlahla became a ward
of Jongintaba at the Great Place in Mqhekezweni1.

Hearing the elders’ stories of his ancestors’ valour during the wars of resistance, he dreamed also
of making his own contribution to the freedom struggle of his people.
